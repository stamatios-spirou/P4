Background:








The Nissan GT-R started off in 1969 as the Skyline GT-R, setting of its motorsport fame. The R32 GT-R launched in 1989, it was nicknamed "Godzilla" due its dominance, featuring a 2.6L twin-turbo engine and AWD. The R33 was produced in 1995, followed by the R34 in 1999 which was a major hit. In 2007, the R35 GT-R redefined performance with a 3.8L twin-turbo V6 and cutting-edge technology, earning its status as a global icon of speed and innovation.
